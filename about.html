About

Where curiosity meets code, and conventional thinking gets left behind.

The Playground

Stray Station is my personal laboratory for exploring AI, building experimental applications, and learning to leverage the technology that's reshaping our future. This isn't a portfolio of perfection: it's a chronicle of curiosity.

As a supply chain and operations executive with over 20 years of leadership experience, I've spent my career optimizing complex systems, driving integration strategies, and delivering measurable results. But somewhere between P&L reviews and transformational roadmaps, I found that my peers, business leaders, and even I lacked a full understanding of technology's trajectory; more importantly, how to value and leverage it to stay ahead of the paradigm and push beyond the possibility frontier.

Every company I encounter talks of innovation and the future. Few actually do it. Their leaders are comfortable going with the flow, getting rewarded for stepping just outside the box while still holding onto it. My mind didn't want that, but I was seeing myself becoming exactly that: comfortably thriving in the current. But even a dead fish can go with the flow.

I needed a forcing mechanism to stop being the dead fish. So I built this station. A place where desire meets curiosity, elation collides with tension, wonder wrestles with anxiety. A place to experiment, to break things, to learn by doing rather than waiting for permission.

The Contrarian Ethos

The name "Stray" isn't accidental. It was given to me by some who know me best. It represents a deliberate departure from the beaten path. While others consume AI through polished interfaces, I'm here to understand what's under the hood. While peers delegate technology to IT departments, I'm learning to build it myself.

This is continuous improvement thinking applied to personal growth: identify the bottleneck (my own technical literacy), design the solution (hands-on learning), execute relentlessly, and iterate based on results.

What You'll Find Here

The Lab showcases my experiments: apps that blend practical utility with creative exploration. Some solve real problems. Others exist simply because I wondered "what if?" Each project teaches me something new about AI, development, or the intersection of technology and human experience.

This is me, learning in public. No corporate polish. No pretense of expertise. Just a professionally curious executive figuring out how to build the future, one project at a time.
