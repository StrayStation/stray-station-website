The Architecture

Two Tracks. One System.

Ideal results require ideal behaviors — and your process and systems drive that behavior. Throughline runs two parallel tracks that reflect exactly that.

Goal Track
The Ideal Results
Cascades from your annual goal down through quarterly milestones, weekly commitments, and the Daily 3. Every action is traceable to a vision that matters.
Non-Negotiables Track
The Ideal Behaviors
Runs in parallel. These are the recurring behaviors that make goals inevitable rather than hopeful. Not tasks — commitments. They don't cascade. They anchor.
The Cascade
True North
Your Personal Vision Statement
Set at onboarding. Permanent anchor. Everything else flows from here. Never changes unless you've fundamentally changed what winning looks like.
Annual Goal
What Winning Looks Like This Year
Four-question structure captures the goal, the arrival picture, the evidence, and the measure. Guided or manual — same output either way.
Quarterly Milestone
What Needs to Be True in 90 Days
Default planning cadence. Week X of 13 shown for orientation. Monthly bucketing is visual only — no additional planning layer to manage.
Weekly Commitments
What Needs to Happen This Week
3–5 across all active goals combined. Outcome-framed — not what you'll work on, but what will be different by Friday.
Daily 3
Three Things That Don't Slip Today
Forced constraint. Always three. Each linked to a weekly commitment. Everything after is priority four and beyond — get to it, delegate it, or defer it. But these three move.
Founding Prompt — True North
A year from now, what would need to be true for you to feel like you made it count?
